Space Invaders.

Embbeded Systmes

The aim is to recreate the game Space Invaders on a PYNQ FPGA board by developing a hardware-software interface. I used C and Assembly language to design device-driver, monitor-debugger, and real-time kernel. I also used C++ for the game portion of the software because of its object-oriented capabilities. The game is simple: A ship that shoots bullets to an array of aliens that moves zigzag across the screen until it reaches the bunkers. At the end of the game the scores are displayed and the top ten best scores are saved. My current stage of development is to write kernel drivers to add sound to the game, and a Programmable Interval Timer

The outcome and how this can benefit you and me:

I can manage a system design project that interfaces digital hardware components to an embedded system processor. I am also able to read and interpret device specifications (data sheets) in order to integrate a device within a microprocessor based system. I can also design and implement a device driver that can be used to interface a device to a microprocessor based system (MAYBE??).

UI/UX DESIGN

I worked with a team to construct a complex, multiplayer laser-tag system. Like most laser-tag system the main purpose of the game is to shoot laser bean to the photodetectors that are on the opponent’s body. The system utilizes a simple optical communication link where each player team is assigned a frequency. The laser pistol turns the LED on and off at rate assigned, then the target converts the received signal into digital signal. The signal then passes through bandpass filters and finally a software determines if the target was hit and by which player team.

How this can benefit you and me?

I was able to implement my knowledge in analog designs by providing the shot-firing LED, shot-detecting photo-diode and associated analog electronics to interface with the FPGA board. In addition to this, I provide the signal-processing necessary to detect when a player has been shot and also to determine which player was the shooter. Further, I reinforce my ability to define, understand, and participate in team decision making processes. Finally, in this experience I was able to take high level customer requirements, identify from them the specifications a solution must meet, and formulate those specifications as an engineering problem.
